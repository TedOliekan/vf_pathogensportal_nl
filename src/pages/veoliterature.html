Introduction: Literature

This page provides access to a selection of publications and supplementary sources within the VEO project that have generated key data-related outputs, including code, models, platforms, databases, and datasets.

Available Publications, Preprints and Supplementary Sources from VEO

Visontai et al., 2019

Kooplex: collaborative data analytics portal for advancing sciences

Reframes scientific data analysis as a fully collaborative and reproducible process by embedding computation, data, and sharing into one unified platform.

Deutsch et al., 2024

ViralPrimer: a web server to monitor viral nucleic acid amplification tests’ primer efficiency during pandemics, with emphasis on SARS-CoV-2 and Mpox

Introduces new computational algorithms for analyzing metagenomic network data.

Rahman et al., 2024

Mobilisation and analyses of publicly available SARS-CoV-2 data for pandemic responses

Shows that pandemic response can be transformed from fragmented efforts into coordinated, data-driven surveillance through scalable open-data infrastructures, though it implicitly depends on sustained global data sharing.

Mentes et al., 2022

Identification of mutations in SARS-CoV-2 PCR primer regions

Demonstrates at scale that viral evolution can directly compromise PCR diagnostics, turning test reliability into a moving target.

Kilim et al., 2023

SARS-CoV-2 receptor-binding domain deep mutational AlphaFold2 structures

Provides curated and standardized datasets for pathogen surveillance and analysis.

Durazzi et al., 2021

Clusters of science and health related Twitter users become more isolated during the COVID-19 pandemic

Analysis of Twitter user clusters discussing science and health topics during the pandemic.

Durazzi et al., 2023

Dynamics of social media behavior pre- and post-SARS-CoV-2 infection

It demonstrates that large-scale social media data can function as a real-time, population-level signal for disease progression and surveillance.

Durazzi et al., 2025

Language models learn to represent antigenic properties of human influenza A(H3) virus

Demonstrates that AI language models can translate viral sequence data into functional antigenic insight, bridging genomics and vaccine decision-making.

Durazzi et al., 2023

Modelling antimicrobial resistance transmission to guide personalized antimicrobial stewardship interventions and infection control policies in healthcare setting: a pilot study

Shows that antimicrobial resistance control can be personalized by linking transmission dynamics to individual patient data.

Becsei et al., 2025

A case study using sewage metagenomic data for assessment of text-to-SQL capabilities in large language models

Explores seasonal trends and source attribution using sewage metagenomics data.

Becsei et al., 2024

Time-series sewage metagenomics distinguishes seasonal, human-derived and environmental microbial communities potentially allowing source-attributed surveillance

Demonstrates that wastewater sequencing can move from bulk monitoring to source-resolved surveillance by separating overlapping microbial signals in time.

Hozé et al., 2025

RSero: A user-friendly R package to reconstruct pathogen circulation history from seroprevalence studies

RSero is an R package that applies Bayesian serocatalytic models to age-stratified seroprevalence data to estimate the force of infection and reconstruct past pathogen transmission dynamics and outbreak history.

Hozé et al., 2025

Reconstructing Mayaro virus circulation in French Guiana shows frequent spillovers

Models serological data to show that Mayaro virus mainly circulates in wildlife with occasional spillover to humans, highlighting the importance of accounting for cross-reactivity.

Hozé et al., 2025

Model-based assessment of Chikungunya and O’nyong-nyong virus circulation in Mali in a serological cross-reactivity context

Uses serological modelling to disentangle chikungunya and O’nyong-nyong virus transmission in Mali, showing that both viruses have circulated with distinct dynamics and that accounting for cross-reactivity is essential for accurate inference.

O’Driscoll et al., 2025

Epidemiological and antigenic inferences from serological cross-reactivity among arboviruses

Develops a framework to analyse multipathogen serological data, enabling joint inference of infection prevalence, transmission history, and antigenic relationships while accounting for antibody cross-reactivity.

Baxter & Yang et al., 2025

The Winners Take It All? Evolutionary Success of H5Nx Reassortants in the 2020–2024 Panzootic

Analyzes the evolutionary success and geographic spread of H5Nx avian influenza reassortants during the 2020–2024 panzootic, highlighting how frequent reassortment and host range expansion have shaped viral adaptation and transmission risk across species.

Harvey et al., 2025

Genetic reassortment and host specificity in H5N1 lineages

Demonstrates that host‑specific reassortment has been a major driver of the evolution and spread of panzootic H5N1 viruses.

Worp et al., 2025

Unveiling the global urban virome through wastewater metagenomics

Shows that global wastewater sequencing can uncover diverse viral communities and enable early detection of emerging pathogens.
